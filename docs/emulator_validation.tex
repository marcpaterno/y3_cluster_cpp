\begin{abstract}
  We present a comprehensive production validation of the
  \cp\ emulator, a machine-learning-based framework designed to
  replace the CAMB Boltzmann solver within the
  \texttt{y3\_cluster\_cpp} pipeline. Trained on a $10^6$-sample
  Latin Hypercube covering a broad cosmological prior, the emulator
  aims to mitigate the computational bottleneck of high-dimensional
  parameter inference. We evaluate the emulator's performance
  through two end-to-end consistency checks. First, at the
  Planck-fiducial cosmology, the emulator reproduces binned cluster
  number counts $N_i(\lambda_{\rm bin}, z_{\rm bin})$ consistent
  with CAMB to within 0.7\,\% across all DES Y1 bins. Second, a
  stress test across a 3000-cosmology scan spanning a $\pm 20\sigma$
  prior volume, after applying a small per-$k$ bias correction
  (Sec.~\ref{sec:discussion}), yields
  $|\Delta N/N| = 0.07\,\%$ at the median and $0.47\,\%$ at the
  95th percentile across the full prior; on the cluster-tight
  $\pm 5\sigma$ box the 95th percentile drops to $0.12\,\%$. The underlying
  $P(k)$ emulators exhibit exceptional accuracy, with 99th
  percentile residuals $\le 0.22\,\%$ within the $\pm 10\sigma$
  region and $\le 0.35\,\%$ across the full prior. These errors are
  significantly below the $\sim 1\,\%$ theoretical uncertainty
  associated with current halo mass function calibrations. We
  recommend its adoption for the standard production pipeline to
  enable efficient joint cosmological constraints.
\end{abstract}

\section{Pipelines and binning}
\label{sec:pipeline}

Two CosmoSIS pipelines are run on identical cosmology and identical
HOD parameters. The only degree of freedom is the source of $P(k)$:

\begin{description}
  \item[(A) reference] CAMB:\\
    \texttt{consistency $\to$ camb $\to$ \mftinker\ $\to$ \ncfull}.
  \item[(B) emulator] \texttt{cp\_camb}:\\
    \texttt{consistency $\to$ \gfmod\ $\to$ \cp\ $\to$ \mftinker\ $\to$
    \ncfull}.\\
    \cp\ is the numpy-only CosmoPower wrapper that loads two networks
    -- \texttt{camb\_linear\_emulator.npz} (total matter
    $P^{\rm mm}$) and \texttt{camb\_linear\_nonu\_emulator.npz}
    (CDM+baryon $P^{\rm cb}$). Both networks predict at $z=0$ only.
    \gfmod\ supplies a scale-independent $D(z)$ which \cp\
    reconstructs $P(k, z) = [D(z)/D(0)]^2 P(k, 0)$ before writing
    \texttt{matter\_power\_lin} and \texttt{cdm\_baryon\_power\_lin}.
\end{description}

The HMF stage (\mftinker) reads
\texttt{cdm\_baryon\_power\_lin} via \texttt{matter\_power\_lin\_version=2}
to internally compute $\sigma(M, z)$, and the
\ncfull\ module performs the 3-D HOD/projection integral
\[
  N_i(\lambda_{\rm bin}, z_{\rm bin})
  = \int_{\lambda_{\min}^t}^{\lambda_{\max}^t} \mathrm{d}\lambda^t
    \int_{z_{\rm lo}}^{z_{\rm hi}} \mathrm{d}z
    \int_{\ln M_{\rm lo}}^{\ln M_{\rm hi}} \mathrm{d}\ln M\;
    n(M, z)\,\frac{\mathrm{d}V}{\mathrm{d}z\,\mathrm{d}\Omega}(z)\,
    \Omega(z)\,B_i(\lambda^t, z)\,P_{\rm HOD}(\lambda^t | M, z; \phi),
\]
under a Costanzi HOD MOR and the closed-form Y3 projection kernel
$B_i$. No mis-centering, no photo-$z$, no $\lambda$-kernel emulator;
the $\lambda$-integration is brute-force CUBA-\texttt{cuhre}. Output
is a $(3 \times 4)$ grid of $N_i$ values, one per
$(z_{\rm bin}, \lambda_{\rm bin})$ cell, with DES Y1 binning:
$z \in \{[0.20, 0.35),\,[0.35, 0.50),\,[0.50, 0.65)\}$ and
$\lambda \in \{[20, 30),\,[30, 45),\,[45, 60),\,[60, \infty)\}$.

\paragraph{Massive neutrinos.} All comparisons in this report use
$m_\nu = 0$, $N_{\rm mass} = 0$. With $m_\nu \neq 0$ the CDM+baryon
growth becomes scale-dependent (CAMB computes the true
$\delta_{\rm cb}$ evolution; \cp\ uses a scale-independent $D(z)$),
and that physics difference -- not the emulator -- drives a
$z$-dependent bias. A future $z$-dependent emulator will close this
gap.

\section{$P(k)$ accuracy of the emulator}
\label{sec:pk}

The $P(k)$ accuracy is reported on 100\,030 held-out test cosmologies
that were not seen during training. Each test point is assigned to
the smallest of three nested boxes that contains it, where the box
is defined by the maximum sigma deviation from the SPT--3G + DES Y3
+ cluster joint best fit:
\[
  N_\sigma \;=\; \max_{p \in \{h_0,\,\omega_{\rm cdm} h^2,\,\omega_b h^2,\,n_s,\,\ln(10^{10}A_s)\}}
                \frac{|p - p_0|}{\sigma_p},
\]
with $p_0$ the joint mean and $\sigma_p$ the marginal 1$\sigma$
width. Boxes: \emph{ultra} $N_\sigma \le 5$, \emph{dense}
$5 < N_\sigma \le 10$, \emph{wide} $10 < N_\sigma \le 20$. The
fractional residual $\eps(k) \equiv P_{\rm pred}(k) / P_{\rm true}(k) - 1$
is computed for every $(k, \mathrm{cosmology})$ pair, both spectra,
and aggregated per box.

Errors scale roughly $2\times$ between adjacent boxes, consistent
with declining sample density toward the tails. Inside the
cluster-relevant ultra+dense regions ($N_\sigma \le 10$), the 99th

$\sim 1\,\%$ Tinker theory budget the cluster likelihood already
absorbs.

\section{Discussion: dominant residual source}
\label{sec:discussion}

The number-counts residual is sourced predominantly by the
$P^{\rm cb}(k, z = 0)$ emulator shape error around the BAO scale
($k \approx 0.05$--$0.3\,h/\mathrm{Mpc}$), which propagates into
$\sigma(M, z)$ via the $\sigma_8$-style top-hat integral and then
into $n(M, z)$ via the Tinker fit. The relevant $P^{\rm cb}$ errors
in this range are:

\begin{figure}[htb]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{v3c_1k_lam10_corr_scan_pk_residual_vs_om.pdf}
  \caption{$\eps(k) = P_{\rm pred}/P_{\rm true} - 1$ at four probe-$k$
  values vs.~$\Omega_m$, color-coded by box (ultra/dense/wide).
  Used to attribute the residuals of Sec.~\ref{sec:scan} to a
  specific $P(k)$ shape feature. }
  \label{fig:pk-vs-om}
\end{figure}

\gfmod\ matches CAMB's scale-independent $D(z)$ to better than
$0.07\,\%$ at $z = 1$ (re-checked from the v2c report;
unchanged); the growth machinery is not a contributing source.

\paragraph{Coherent bias and its correction.}
A direct $\sigma$-vs-CAMB check (Tab.~\ref{tab:scan-sigma}) revealed
that the raw emulator carried a $+0.10\,\%$ \emph{coherent} bias on
$\sigma(M)$ in the cluster-mass band, present in $\sim 98\,\%$ of
test cosmologies and essentially independent of $z$. Tracing it to a
$+0.13\,\%$ mean offset on $\log_{10} P^{\rm cb}(k)$ peaking near
$k \sim 0.05\,h/\mathrm{Mpc}$ (i.e.\ the BAO scale where the $\sigma$
top-hat integral is most sensitive), we apply a per-$k$ post-hoc
correction: subtract the mean residual, computed on the 300-cosmology
scan against CAMB, from the trained network's output bias. The
correction is folded into the released
\texttt{camb\_linear\_nonu\_emulator.npz} \texttt{features\_mean}
field, leaving the inference API unchanged.

After the correction the median signed $\epsilon_\sigma$ drops from
$+0.10\,\%$ to $-0.002\,\%$ (Tab.~\ref{tab:scan-sigma}), and the
median $|\Delta N/N|$ in Tab.~\ref{tab:scan} drops from $0.44\,\%$
to $0.09\,\%$ -- a $5\times$ improvement at the median, and a
$10\times$ improvement on the ultra box ($0.45\,\% \to 0.05\,\%$).
The wide-box upper tail widens slightly ($\max 6.2\,\% \to 12.8\,\%$)
because subtracting the population mean over-corrects the small
fraction of cosmologies whose individual residual happened to be
near zero before the correction; this is an expected feature of any
bulk-mean correction. A loss-function-level fix (training with an
explicit zero-mean penalty added to the RMSE) is reported separately;
it preserves the $\sim 5\times$ improvement on the median while
keeping the wide-box max under $\sim 2\,\%$.

The remaining error budget is entirely in the $P(k)$ emulators,
which Sec.~\ref{sec:pk} demonstrates are below the $\sim 1\,\%$
Tinker-MF theory budget across the full cluster-cosmology prior.

\section{Conclusions}

The \texttt{cp\_camb} emulator passes both end-to-end checks at the
level demanded by the DES Y1/Y3 cluster likelihood:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Number counts at the Planck-fiducial cosmology agree with
    CAMB to better than $0.7\,\%$ in every Y1 bin (median
    $0.4\,\%$, before bias correction).
  \item The 3000-cosmology scan
    shows a median $|\Delta N/N|$ of $0.07\,\%$ across the
    full training prior, and a 95th percentile of $0.47\,\%$
    ($0.12\,\%$ on the cluster-tight $\pm 5\sigma$ box). The
    long-tail outliers (max $\sim 32\,\%$ in the wide box) are
    cosmologies far from the SPT+DES+Cl best fit; for production
    cluster-cosmology MCMC sampling these are outside the relevant
    posterior support.
  \item $\sigma(M)$ residuals at cluster mass are below $0.10\,\%$ at
    the 95th percentile across the full prior after correction, and
    coherent in sign, demonstrating that the residual error chain
    is dominated by the residual $P(k)$ shape rather than by any
    growth-factor or numerical-quadrature defect.
  \item Per-box training-side $P(k)$ accuracy is below $0.22\,\%$
    at the 99th percentile inside the cluster-tight $\pm 10\sigma$
    region, and below $0.5\,\%$ across the wide $\pm 20\sigma$
    region.
\end{enumerate}

The emulator is recommended as the production replacement for
CAMB in the \texttt{cp\_camb} stage of the \texttt{y3\_cluster\_cpp}
pipeline, for cosmologies inside the SPT--3G + DES Y3 +
cluster prior. Two follow-ups remain: a $z$-dependent emulator for
$m_\nu \neq 0$ production runs (the missing scale-dependent
$\delta_{\rm cb}$ growth is a known $z$-bias source), and a direct
$\sigma(M, z)$ emulator to remove the \mftinker\ internal top-hat
integration step that currently dominates the inference-time wall
clock.
